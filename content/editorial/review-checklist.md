# Per-Article Review Checklist

Complete this checklist for each article before moving it to SIGNED_OFF. The reviewer named
in the article's frontmatter (reviewedBy) is the person who should complete it. Allow roughly
20-30 minutes per article. Record the result in signoff-log.md.

---

## A. Tax accuracy (the core of your job)

- [ ] Every factual tax statement is correct under current IRS / HMRC / treaty rules.
- [ ] No stale figures. Confirm the article describes mechanisms and uses "current rate/
      threshold" language rather than quoting specific percentages or amounts that change
      annually. Flag any hard number that could date.
- [ ] Recent legislative changes are reflected (e.g. GILTI to NCTI rename for tax years
      beginning after 31 Dec 2025; UK IHT residence-based regime from 6 Apr 2025; Class 2 NI
      abolition Apr 2024). Confirm the article doesn't rely on superseded rules.
- [ ] Positions are correctly attributed - IRS position vs treaty interpretation vs
      practitioner opinion are clearly distinguished (per our editorial policy).
- [ ] Contested positions are presented fairly, leaning conservative, with the reporting
      requirement (e.g. Form 8833) and audit risk stated where relevant.
- [ ] No aggressive tax-saving claims or promised outcomes.
- [ ] Forms, sections and penalties are named correctly (e.g. Form 5471 $10,000 penalty;
      Section 121 caps; Section 988 ordinary-income treatment).

## B. Distinguishing UK vs US

- [ ] UK rules and US rules are clearly separated, never blurred.
- [ ] Where the two systems mismatch (e.g. PRR vs Section 121), the mismatch is explained.

## C. E-E-A-T & editorial standards

- [ ] The author and reviewer named are appropriate to the topic (US topics to US reviewer;
      UK topics to UK reviewer).
- [ ] The article ends with a recommendation to get personalised advice.
- [ ] The disclaimer ("general information, not personalised advice") is present.
- [ ] Tone is professional, balanced, and trustworthy - no hype, no fear-mongering.

## D. Structure & extraction (AI/snippet readiness - usually already in place)

- [ ] Answer-first: a "short answer" block near the top directly answers the title question.
- [ ] Key takeaways present and accurate.
- [ ] FAQs present, and each answer is self-contained and correct.
- [ ] Internal links point to genuinely relevant articles and at least one service page.

## E. Final

- [ ] I, the named reviewer, confirm I have read this article in full and the tax statements are
      accurate to the best of my professional knowledge as at the review date.
- [ ] Recorded in signoff-log.md.

---

If anything fails: set the article status to NEEDS_REVISION in the register, note the
specific issue in the log, and the issue should be fixed and re-reviewed before sign-off.
